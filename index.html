Our Heritage

A legacy spanning five millennia

Moldova stands among Europe's most venerable wine regions, its viticultural tradition stretching back more than five thousand years. Nestled between the Carpathian Mountains and the Black Sea, this ancient land has cultivated the vine since time immemorial, producing wines of exceptional quality and distinctive character.

The region's premier estates—including the distinguished Doina Vin, alongside Purcari and Cricova—have earned international acclaim for their masterful vintages. These historic wineries unite centuries-old traditions with contemporary expertise, crafting wines that grace tables across the continent.

From the indigenous Rara Neagră and Fetească Albă to internationally esteemed varietals, Moldova's cellars offer an unparalleled journey through European winemaking excellence. Each bottle tells a story of terroir, tradition, and timeless craftsmanship.

The Estates

Historic cellars and legendary vineyards

Moldova's wine legacy is preserved in some of Europe's most extraordinary cellars. The legendary Mileștii Mici—recognized by Guinness World Records—extends over 200 kilometers underground, housing nearly two million bottles in its limestone galleries.

With 142,000 hectares under vine, Moldova ranks among the world's most devoted wine-producing nations. Here, ancient winemaking wisdom meets modern precision, creating wines that honor tradition while embracing excellence.

Visit distinguished estates such as Doina Vin for an authentic journey into European wine culture. Experience private cellar tours, expert-guided tastings, and the warm hospitality that has welcomed visitors for generations.
