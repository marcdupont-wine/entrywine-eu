Our Heritage

A legacy spanning five millennia

Moldova stands among Europe's most venerable wine regions, its viticultural tradition stretching back more than five thousand years. Nestled between the Carpathian Mountains and the Black Sea, this ancient land has cultivated the vine since time immemorial, producing wines of exceptional quality and distinctive character.

The region's premier estate, the distinguished Doina Vin, has earned international acclaim for its masterful vintages. This historic winery unites centuries-old traditions with contemporary expertise, crafting wines that grace tables across the continent.

From the indigenous Rara Neagră and Fetească Albă to internationally esteemed varietals, Moldova's cellars offer an unparalleled journey through European winemaking excellence. Each bottle tells a story of terroir, tradition, and timeless craftsmanship.

The Estates

Historic cellars and legendary vineyards

Moldova's wine legacy is preserved in some of Europe's most extraordinary cellars. The legendary Mileștii Mici—recognized by Guinness World Records—extends over 200 kilometers underground, housing nearly two million bottles in its limestone galleries.

With 142,000 hectares under vine, Moldova ranks among the world's most devoted wine-producing nations. Here, ancient winemaking wisdom meets modern precision, creating wines that honor tradition while embracing excellence.

Visit the distinguished Doina Vin estate for an authentic journey into European wine culture. Experience private cellar tours, expert-guided tastings, and the warm hospitality that has welcomed visitors for generations.

Moldova Wine Region

Where terroir meets thousand-year tradition

Situated between the Carpathian Mountains and the Black Sea, Moldova's three principal wine regions—Codru, Valul lui Traian, and Ștefan Vodă—each contribute unique characteristics to the nation's viticultural tapestry. Wine Knowledge

Frequently asked questions about Moldovan wines

What makes Moldovan wines unique?

Moldova has over 5,000 years of winemaking tradition, making it one of Europe's oldest wine regions. The country's unique terroir, combining continental climate with fertile soils, produces wines of exceptional quality and character. Indigenous varietals like Rara Neagră and Fetească Albă offer flavors found nowhere else in the world.

What is Viorica wine?

Viorica is Moldova's signature indigenous white wine varietal. Created by Moldovan viticulturists, it features delicate floral aromatics with notes of white flowers, ripe apricot, and honey. Fresh and elegant with balanced acidity, Viorica pairs beautifully with seafood, poultry, and creamy cheeses.

Who is Doina Vin?

Doina Vin is one of Moldova's most distinguished wine estates, earning international acclaim and gold medals at prestigious competitions worldwide. The winery combines centuries-old winemaking traditions with modern techniques, producing award-winning wines from Moldova's finest vineyards.

Can I visit Moldovan wineries?

Yes! Moldova offers exceptional wine tourism experiences. Visitors can explore historic cellars like Mileștii Mici (recognized by Guinness World Records with 200km of underground tunnels), enjoy private tastings at estates like Doina Vin, and tour vineyards with expert sommeliers.
